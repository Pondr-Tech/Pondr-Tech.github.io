About Us

Kenneth Ge

Kenny's coding journey began when he was nine, and wanted to recreate Minecraft. Yeah, his journey started just like any other. But soon, he began to discover the sheer power that coding has to do all sorts of awesome stuff, and he started by coding up various Java games and Android apps, including Complex Animator, his first major release. He then went on to study algorithms in the USA Computing Olympiad, ultimately qualifying for Platinum in December 2020. As of now, he curates his pages on GitHub and and itch.io, where he publishes various games and projects relating to his favorite topics in CS: computer networking, algorithms, and computer graphics. These projects often come from the Programming Club, where he teaches advanced CS projects every Thursday.

In his free time, Kenny loves to compose music (Spotify), go out into nature, and get cinnamon pretzels with friends.

Mihir Patil

Mihir started coding from Scratch (literally and figuratively) in fourth grade, and learned Python soon after. After getting a Raspberry Pi kit in sixth grade he, worked on projects including automating his home, and creating a self-driving car model. At school, he runs the Raspberry Pi Club, which helps introduce 7-12 graders to physical computing. He loves to compete in hackathons, which are his favorite way to learn new technology. You can find him at his GitHub page.

When he isn't coding, he enjoys playing biking, playing video games, and playing the piano

Parker Rho

Parker has always had an interest in teaching, so Pondr Tech was a naturaly way to express and explore his passion in education. He loved that a single passionate individual like him could design and create an entire educational nonprofit -- complete with online courses and a camp -- from scratch. His favorite moment from our Raspberry Pi camp with Urban Legacy was being able to see the concepts click physically and obviously in our mentees. These moments, though small, are immensely fulfilling to him because of the impact he can make on the world.

Parker loves seeing how everything in the virtual world interacts and playing with them in a separate dimension to the real world. (Although, he loves building and tinkering in the Robotics Club, too.)

In his free time, Parker loves tennis, video games, reading fantasy novels (currently obsessed with Lord of the Rings) and eating and making Korean food.
